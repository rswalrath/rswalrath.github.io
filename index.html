Rowan Walrath

Multimedia Journalist

Now

These days, I’m a Ben Bagdikian online editorial fellow in Mother Jones' San Francisco office. My beat sticks mainly to science and tech, but I’ll write about whatever captures my interest.

I also do multimedia stuff. I’m a slight hackathon/conference junkie. Plus, I’ve been known to design websites (like this one) and produce the occasional video.

Then

Before the Bay Area, I lived in the Bay State. The city of Boston gave me my foundation in community reporting. Over four years, I learned to write fast, sometimes long, and always with an eye toward the bigger picture. I haven't lost sight of what makes local news so great.

My bylines live on in the Boston Globe, Boston Magazine, and WGBH News, among other publications.

Future

Education and community drive me, and for me, journalism is an in-the-moment research opportunity. Turns out people are more likely to answer my questions if I preface them with "I’m Rowan, a reporter with XYZ Publication."

Please hit me up for writing, editing, audience engagement, multimedia and web production — and whatever cool future-of-media thing you're secretly dreaming up.
